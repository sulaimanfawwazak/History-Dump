## 1889-03-31

On March 31, 1889, the iconic Eiffel Tower was officially inaugurated in Paris, France. Designed by engineer Gustave Eiffel, this wrought-iron lattice tower served as the grand entrance to the 1889 World's Fair, Exposition Universelle. Its construction was a remarkable feat, completed in just over two years, two months, and five days. Standing at 324 meters (1,063 ft), it briefly held the title of the world's tallest man-made structure. Although originally intended to be a temporary exhibit, its value for radio transmission ensured its permanence, solidifying its status as a global symbol of France.
