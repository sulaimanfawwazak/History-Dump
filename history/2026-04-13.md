# Jallianwala Bagh Massacre

![Jallianwala Bagh Massacre](https://www.vifindia.org/sites/default/files/Jallianwala-Bagh-Massacre-13-4-1919.jpg)

## 1919-04-13

On April 13, 1919, a large, peaceful gathering of unarmed Indian civilians converged at Jallianwala Bagh in Amritsar, Punjab, British India. The crowd, consisting of men, women, and children, was there to protest the repressive Rowlatt Act and to celebrate the Sikh festival of Baisakhi. Brigadier-General Reginald Dyer ordered his troops to open fire on the trapped crowd without warning, continuing for approximately 10 minutes until their ammunition was almost exhausted. Official British figures reported 379 deaths, but Indian sources estimated the casualties to be over 1,000 killed and thousands more wounded. This horrific act became a turning point in India's struggle for independence, igniting widespread outrage and intensifying the nationalist movement against British rule.
