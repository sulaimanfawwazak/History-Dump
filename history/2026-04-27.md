# South Africa Holds First Non-Racial Democratic Election

![South Africa Holds First Non-Racial Democratic Election](https://lookaside.fbsbx.com/lookaside/crawler/media/?media_id=1382694050544863)

## 1994-04-27

On April 27, 1994, South Africa conducted its inaugural fully non-racial democratic election, a pivotal moment marking the official end of apartheid. Millions of previously disenfranchised citizens, predominantly black South Africans, cast their votes for the first time in the nation's history. This historic election led to a landslide victory for the African National Congress (ANC) and subsequently saw Nelson Mandela elected as the country's first black president. The event symbolized a peaceful transition of power and was celebrated globally as a triumph of democracy and human rights.
