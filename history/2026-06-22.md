## 1941-06-22

On June 22, 1941, Nazi Germany launched Operation Barbarossa, a massive and surprise invasion of the Soviet Union during World War II. This offensive, violating the Molotov–Ribbentrop Pact, involved over three million Axis soldiers along a vast front from the Baltic Sea to the Black Sea. The invasion marked a dramatic escalation, opening the Eastern Front, which would become the largest and most brutal theater of conflict in human history. Despite initial rapid advances deep into Soviet territory, the German offensive ultimately stalled due to fierce Soviet resistance, the onset of harsh winter conditions, and logistical overextension. This pivotal turning point greatly contributed to the eventual defeat of Nazi Germany.
