# Neil Armstrong Becomes First Man to Walk on Moon

## 1969-07-21

On July 21, 1969, American astronaut Neil Armstrong made history by becoming the first human to walk on the Moon. Stepping out of the Apollo 11 Lunar Module "Eagle," he famously declared, "That's one small step for [a] man, one giant leap for mankind." This monumental achievement, watched by an estimated 600 million people worldwide, marked the successful culmination of the "Space Race" and President John F. Kennedy's challenge to land a man on the Moon before the decade's end. Armstrong was joined on the lunar surface by Buzz Aldrin, and they spent over two hours exploring before returning to the module. This event cemented a new era of human exploration and scientific discovery.
