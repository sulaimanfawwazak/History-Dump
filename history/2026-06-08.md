## 1967-06-08

On June 8, 1967, during the Six-Day War, the USS Liberty, a U.S. Navy technical research ship, was attacked by Israeli air and naval forces in international waters off the Sinai Peninsula. The sustained assault, involving fighter jets and torpedo boats, killed 34 American servicemen and wounded 171 others. Israel claimed the attack was a case of mistaken identity, believing the ship to be an Egyptian vessel. However, the U.S. and Israeli investigations, while differing on details, both concluded it was an error, a conclusion that remains controversial among some U.S. officials and survivors who believe the attack was deliberate.
