# Operation Entebbe: Israeli Hostage Rescue Mission

![Operation Entebbe: Israeli Hostage Rescue Mission](https://lieber.westpoint.edu/wp-content/uploads/2024/07/Operation-Summer-Seeds-0001.jpg)

## 1976-07-03

On July 3, 1976, Israeli commandos launched a daring rescue mission, code-named "Operation Entebbe," to free over 100 predominantly Israeli and Jewish hostages held by Palestinian and German terrorists at Entebbe Airport in Uganda. The terrorists had hijacked Air France Flight 139 from Tel Aviv to Paris a week earlier, diverting it to Uganda. Traveling over 2,500 miles, the elite Sayeret Matkal unit successfully stormed the airport terminal, eliminating all terrorists and dozens of Ugandan soldiers. The raid resulted in the deaths of three hostages, all the hijackers, and one Israeli commando, Yonatan Netanyahu, but successfully rescued 102 hostages, becoming one of the most audacious and successful hostage rescue operations in history.
