# The State of Israel Declares Independence

## 1948-05-14

On May 14, 1948, the Provisional State Council of Israel declared the establishment of the State of Israel in Tel Aviv, just hours before the British Mandate for Palestine was set to expire. David Ben-Gurion, who would become Israel's first Prime Minister, read the Declaration of the Establishment of the State of Israel. This momentous declaration marked the culmination of the Zionist movement's efforts to create a Jewish state in its ancient homeland. Immediately following the declaration, neighboring Arab states launched a military invasion, initiating the 1948 Arab–Israeli War. The United States, under President Harry S. Truman, recognized the new state just minutes after its declaration.
