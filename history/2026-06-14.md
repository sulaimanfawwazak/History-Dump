# German Forces Capture Paris During World War II

![German Forces Capture Paris During World War II](https://cdn.britannica.com/78/177078-050-EFCF854A/Adolf-Hitler-front-Paris-Eiffel-Tower-France-June-1940.jpg)

## 1940-06-14

On June 14, 1940, during World War II, German forces marched into and occupied Paris, the capital city of France. This significant event followed weeks of rapid German advancement through France, bypassing the Maginot Line and overwhelming Allied defenses. The French government, under Prime Minister Paul Reynaud, had declared Paris an open city and evacuated to Bordeaux to prevent its destruction, leaving it undefended. The occupation of Paris by Nazi Germany lasted for over four years, profoundly altering the city's daily life and becoming a symbol of French subjugation until its liberation in August 1944.
