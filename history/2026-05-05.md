## 1961-05-05

On May 5, 1961, astronaut Alan Shepard piloted the Freedom 7 capsule, becoming the first American to travel into space. His suborbital flight, part of Project Mercury, lasted 15 minutes and 28 seconds, reaching an altitude of 116 miles (187 km) before splashing down safely in the Atlantic Ocean. This historic achievement came less than a month after Soviet cosmonaut Yuri Gagarin's orbital flight, intensifying the Space Race between the United States and the Soviet Union. Shepard's successful mission boosted American morale and paved the way for future crewed spaceflights, including the eventual Apollo moon landings.
