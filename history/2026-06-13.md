# Miranda v. Arizona Decision

![Miranda v. Arizona Decision](https://www.loc.gov/static/research-centers/law-library-of-congress/images/lib-guides/miranda/384-us-436.png)

## 1966-06-13

On this day, the U.S. Supreme Court delivered its landmark 5-4 decision in Miranda v. Arizona, establishing what became known as "Miranda rights." The ruling held that criminal suspects in police custody must be informed of their constitutional right to an attorney and against self-incrimination before questioning. This crucial decision significantly impacted police procedure across the United States, requiring law enforcement to issue a clear warning to suspects. The case arose from Ernesto Miranda's arrest and conviction for kidnapping and rape in Phoenix, Arizona, where his confession was used against him despite not being informed of his rights. The "Miranda warning" has since become a standard part of arrest procedures nationwide.
