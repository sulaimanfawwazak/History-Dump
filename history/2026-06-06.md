# D-Day: Allied Forces Land in Normandy

![D-Day: Allied Forces Land in Normandy](https://www.nationalww2museum.org/sites/default/files/2017-06/d-day-1920x1400.jpg)

## 1944-06-06

On June 6, 1944, during World War II, the Allied forces launched Operation Overlord, commonly known as D-Day, initiating the largest seaborne invasion in history. More than 156,000 American, British, and Canadian troops landed on five beaches along a 50-mile stretch of heavily fortified coast in Normandy, France, aiming to liberate Western Europe from Nazi Germany's control. The landings, which included coordinated air and naval bombardments, faced fierce German resistance, particularly on Omaha Beach. Despite heavy casualties, the Allies successfully established a critical foothold, opening a crucial second front against Germany. This pivotal event marked a major turning point in the war, ultimately leading to the liberation of France and the eventual defeat of Nazi Germany.
