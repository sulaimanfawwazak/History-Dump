# Indian Removal Act Signed Into Law

![Indian Removal Act Signed Into Law](http://www.nativehistoryassociation.org/images/s102.jpg)

## 1830-05-28

On May 28, 1830, United States President Andrew Jackson signed the Indian Removal Act into law. This controversial legislation authorized the president to negotiate with Native American tribes for their removal from ancestral lands in the southeastern United States to federal territory west of the Mississippi River. The act was a cornerstone of Jackson's policy toward Native Americans, often justified by the desire for white settlement and land for cotton cultivation. Its implementation led to the forced displacement of thousands of Native Americans, most notably through the "Trail of Tears," causing immense suffering and loss of life.
