## 1949-05-12

On May 12, 1949, the Soviet Union formally lifted its nearly year-long blockade of West Berlin, marking a significant early victory for the Western Allies in the Cold War. The blockade, imposed in June 1948, was an attempt by Moscow to force the United States, Great Britain, and France to relinquish their sectors of the divided German capital. In response, the Western powers launched the massive Berlin Airlift, continuously flying in food, fuel, and other essential supplies to the city's two million residents. This unprecedented humanitarian operation delivered over 2.3 million tons of cargo, demonstrating remarkable logistical prowess and unwavering Western resolve. The successful airlift ultimately rendered the blockade ineffective, compelling the Soviets to concede and reopen land and water routes.
