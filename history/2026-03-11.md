# The Great East Japan Earthquake and Tsunami

![The Great East Japan Earthquake and Tsunami](https://cdn.theatlantic.com/media/img/photo/2016/03/5-years-since-the-2011-great-east-j/q01_RTR2JTXO-1/original.jpg)

## 2011-03-11

On March 11, 2011, a massive magnitude 9.1 offshore earthquake struck the Tōhoku region of Japan, generating a series of devastating tsunami waves that reached heights of up to 40.5 meters. The natural disaster caused widespread destruction along Japan's Pacific coast, leading to over 15,000 deaths and triggering a meltdown at the Fukushima Daiichi Nuclear Power Plant, releasing radioactive material. This event is considered one of the costliest natural disasters in history, profoundly impacting the global energy debate and disaster preparedness. The earthquake also shifted the entire main island of Honshu by 2.4 meters and the Earth on its axis by 10 to 25 centimeters.
