## 1894-06-23

On June 23, 1894, the International Olympic Committee (IOC) was officially founded at the Sorbonne in Paris, France. This pivotal moment marked the culmination of efforts by its visionary founder, Baron Pierre de Coubertin, to revive the ancient Olympic Games. Coubertin believed that a modernized version of the games could promote international understanding, peace, and athletic excellence. The committee was established to oversee the organization and development of the new Olympic movement. This initiative led directly to the first modern Olympic Games held in Athens, Greece, in 1896, forever changing the landscape of global sport.
