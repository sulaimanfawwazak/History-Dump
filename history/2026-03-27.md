# Tenerife Airport Disaster Occurs

## 1977-03-27

On March 27, 1977, the deadliest accident in aviation history occurred on the runway of Los Rodeos Airport (now Tenerife North Airport) in Tenerife, Canary Islands, Spain. Two Boeing 747 passenger jets, KLM Flight 4805 and Pan Am Flight 1736, collided in dense fog. The tragic event resulted from a series of miscommunications between air traffic control and the KLM flight crew, compounded by the challenging weather conditions. A total of 583 people perished in the catastrophe, with only 61 passengers and crew members from the Pan Am flight surviving the initial impact. The disaster led to significant changes in international aviation protocols, emphasizing standardized phraseology and improved crew resource management to prevent future incidents.
