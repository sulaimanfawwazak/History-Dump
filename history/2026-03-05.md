## 1770-03-05

On March 5, 1770, a confrontation between British soldiers and a crowd of Bostonian colonists escalated into violence, resulting in the deaths of five colonists. Tensions had been high in Boston due to the presence of British troops occupying the city and enforcing unpopular tax laws. The incident began when a small group of soldiers was harassed by a mob, leading to shots being fired into the crowd. Among those killed was Crispus Attucks, often considered the first casualty of the American Revolution. The Boston Massacre became a pivotal moment in fueling anti-British sentiment and contributing to the growing calls for independence in the American colonies.
