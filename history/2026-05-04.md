# Kent State Shootings

![Kent State Shootings](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/6/65/Kent_State_massacre.jpg)

## 1970-05-04

On May 4, 1970, members of the Ohio National Guard opened fire on unarmed college students at Kent State University in Kent, Ohio, during a protest against the Cambodian Incursion and the Vietnam War. The tragic event resulted in the deaths of four students—Allison Krause, Jeffrey Miller, Sandra Scheuer, and William Schroeder—and the wounding of nine others. The shootings sparked a nationwide student strike, closing hundreds of universities, and further intensified the already polarized debate over the Vietnam War in the United States. It remains a watershed moment in American history, symbolizing the deep divisions and civil unrest of the era.
