# Sinking of the RMS Lusitania

![Sinking of the RMS Lusitania](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/81/Bundesarchiv_DVM_10_Bild-23-61-17%2C_Untergang_der_%22Lusitania%22.jpg)

## 1915-05-07

On May 7, 1915, the British ocean liner RMS Lusitania was sunk by a German U-boat U-20 off the coast of Ireland during World War I. The passenger ship, traveling from New York to Liverpool, was struck by a single torpedo while approaching the Old Head of Kinsale, causing it to sink in just 18 minutes. Of the 1,959 people on board, 1,198 lost their lives, including 128 American citizens. This act of unrestricted submarine warfare caused widespread international outrage, particularly in the United States. While not the sole reason, the sinking significantly influenced public opinion and contributed to the U.S. decision to declare war on Germany two years later.
