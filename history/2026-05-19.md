## 1536-05-19

On May 19, 1536, Anne Boleyn, the second wife of King Henry VIII of England, was executed by beheading at the Tower of London. She had been convicted of treason, adultery, incest, and conspiring to murder the King, though many historians believe these charges were fabricated to allow Henry to marry Jane Seymour. Her execution marked a pivotal and dramatic moment in the English Reformation and the turbulent politics of the Tudor dynasty. Anne's fall from grace led to the legitimization of Henry's marriage to Jane Seymour just days later. Her daughter, Elizabeth I, would later ascend to the throne, becoming one of England's most powerful and influential monarchs.
