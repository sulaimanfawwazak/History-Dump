## 1928-11-18

On November 18, 1928, Walt Disney's iconic character, Mickey Mouse, officially made his public debut in the animated short film "Steamboat Willie." Premiering at the Colony Theater in New York City, this was not Mickey's first animated appearance but notably his first synchronized sound cartoon. Co-directed by Walt Disney and Ub Iwerks, the film marked a significant milestone in animation history with its innovative use of synchronized sound, transforming the medium. The immediate success of "Steamboat Willie" propelled Mickey Mouse into global stardom. He quickly became a cultural phenomenon and an enduring symbol of The Walt Disney Company.
