# Anti-Ballistic Missile Treaty Signed

![Anti-Ballistic Missile Treaty Signed](https://encyclopedia.pub/media/item_content/202505/mceclip0-682ebeb4eeeb6.jpg)

## 1972-05-26

On May 26, 1972, the Anti-Ballistic Missile (ABM) Treaty was signed between the United States and the Soviet Union in Moscow. This landmark agreement, a cornerstone of the Strategic Arms Limitation Talks (SALT I), limited both nations' anti-ballistic missile systems used in defending areas against ballistic missile-delivered nuclear weapons. The treaty aimed to prevent a new arms race in defensive weaponry, thereby maintaining the concept of Mutual Assured Destruction (MAD) and deterring a first strike. It was considered a crucial step in de-escalating Cold War tensions and promoting strategic stability between the two superpowers.
