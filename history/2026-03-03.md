# Treaty of Brest-Litovsk Signed

![Treaty of Brest-Litovsk Signed](https://cdn.britannica.com/85/137285-050-A6ECE458/Delegates-negotiations-treaties-Brest-Litovsk-1918.jpg)

## 1918-03-03

On March 3, 1918, Soviet Russia signed the Treaty of Brest-Litovsk with the Central Powers (Germany, Austria-Hungary, Bulgaria, and the Ottoman Empire) in Brest-Litovsk, effectively ending Russia's participation in World War I. The treaty imposed extremely harsh conditions on Russia, forcing it to cede vast territories, including Poland, Finland, the Baltic states, Ukraine, and parts of Belarus. Bolshevik leader Vladimir Lenin had pushed for peace at any cost to consolidate the new communist government amidst the ongoing Russian Civil War. This significant territorial and economic loss was deeply unpopular within Russia but was deemed necessary by the Bolsheviks to survive. Although the treaty's terms were largely nullified by the Armistice of November 1918, it dramatically reshaped Eastern Europe and allowed Germany to shift troops to the Western Front.
