# Exxon Valdez Oil Spill

![Exxon Valdez Oil Spill](https://hakaimagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/header-exxon-anniversary.jpg)

## 1989-03-24

On March 24, 1989, the oil tanker Exxon Valdez ran aground on Bligh Reef in Prince William Sound, Alaska. This catastrophic event resulted in the spilling of approximately 11 million gallons of crude oil into the pristine waters. The spill devastated the local ecosystem, killing hundreds of thousands of seabirds, sea otters, seals, and other marine wildlife. It remains one of the largest environmental disasters in U.S. history and prompted significant changes in environmental legislation and shipping safety practices. The incident highlighted the severe risks associated with oil transportation and long-term ecological recovery challenges.
