# Tennis Court Oath

![Tennis Court Oath](https://cdn.britannica.com/35/182635-050-43342CDA/Tennis-Court-Oath-Jacques-Louis-David-Louvre-Paris-1791.jpg)

## 1789-06-20

On June 20, 1789, during the early stages of the French Revolution, members of the Third Estate, who had formed the National Assembly, were locked out of their usual meeting hall at the Palace of Versailles. They reconvened in a nearby indoor tennis court. There, they took a collective oath, vowing not to disperse until a new constitution for France had been established and enacted. This pivotal event asserted the Assembly's determination against royal opposition and marked a significant step towards the dismantling of the absolute monarchy.
