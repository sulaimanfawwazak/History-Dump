# United States Senate Rejects Treaty Of Versailles For A Second Time

![United States Senate Rejects Treaty Of Versailles For A Second Time](https://static01.nyt.com/images/2012/03/18/learning/Mar19LN/Mar19LN-blog480.jpg)

## 1920-03-19

On March 19, 1920, the United States Senate rejected the Treaty of Versailles for a second and final time, effectively preventing American participation in the League of Nations. President Woodrow Wilson, a key architect of the treaty and the League, had championed its ratification as essential for global peace and cooperation after World War I. However, a bloc of Republican senators, led by Henry Cabot Lodge, expressed strong concerns over Article X of the League's covenant, fearing it would entangle the U.S. in foreign conflicts without Congressional approval. This rejection marked a significant setback for Wilson's vision and signaled America's return to a more isolationist foreign policy in the interwar period. The U.S. never joined the League of Nations, weakening the international body's effectiveness from its inception.
