# New Hampshire Ratifies U.S. Constitution

![New Hampshire Ratifies U.S. Constitution](https://www.cui.edu/portals/0/uploadedimages/centers_and_institutes/center_for_public_policy/jun22-header.jpg)

## 1788-06-21

On June 21, 1788, New Hampshire became the ninth state to ratify the United States Constitution. This crucial vote, held in Exeter, officially put the Constitution into effect, as Article VII stipulated that nine states were required for its establishment. The ratification marked a significant milestone in the formation of the American federal government, transitioning from the Articles of Confederation to a stronger, more centralized system. Although nine states had ratified, debates continued, and the remaining states eventually followed suit, solidifying the new republic.
