## 1803-04-30

On April 30, 1803, the United States officially acquired the Louisiana Territory from France, an event known as the Louisiana Purchase. This momentous agreement saw the U.S. pay roughly $15 million to Napoleon Bonaparte's government for a vast expanse of land comprising approximately 828,000 square miles. Negotiated by American envoys Robert Livingston and James Monroe in Paris under President Thomas Jefferson's direction, the purchase effectively doubled the size of the young nation. The acquisition was a pivotal moment in American history, securing control of the vital Mississippi River and its port of New Orleans, while also paving the way for westward expansion and exploration.
