# Space Shuttle Columbia Disaster

![Space Shuttle Columbia Disaster](https://cdn.britannica.com/99/85199-050-C99B45D9/David-Brown-space-shuttle-Columbia-Rick-Husband-February-1-2003.jpg)

## 2003-02-01

On February 1, 2003, the Space Shuttle Columbia disintegrated upon re-entry into Earth's atmosphere over Texas and Louisiana, tragically killing all seven astronauts on board. The disaster was later attributed to a piece of foam insulation that broke off the external tank during launch and struck the orbiter's left wing, damaging its thermal protection system. This catastrophic failure allowed superheated atmospheric gases to penetrate the wing structure during re-entry, leading to the shuttle's breakup. The tragedy resulted in a 29-month hiatus in NASA's space shuttle program and a major re-evaluation of safety protocols and mission planning for future space flights, deeply impacting the American space program. This event served as a stark reminder of the inherent risks of space exploration.
