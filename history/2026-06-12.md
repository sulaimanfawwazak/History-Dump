# Loving v. Virginia Decision

![Loving v. Virginia Decision](https://i0.wp.com/prologue.blogs.archives.gov/wp-content/uploads/sites/9/2021/01/692_original.jpg?ssl=1)

## 1967-06-12

On June 12, 1967, the U.S. Supreme Court unanimously ruled in *Loving v. Virginia* that state laws prohibiting interracial marriage were unconstitutional. This landmark civil rights decision overturned antimiscegenation laws in 16 states, effectively legalizing interracial marriage across the United States. The case involved Richard and Mildred Loving, an interracial couple who had been sentenced to a year in prison in Virginia for violating the state's Racial Integrity Act. The Court's ruling, based on the Equal Protection and Due Process Clauses of the Fourteenth Amendment, declared marriage a fundamental right and affirmed racial equality before the law, marking a significant victory for the American civil rights movement.
