# Dolly the Sheep, the First Cloned Mammal, Is Born

![Dolly the Sheep, the First Cloned Mammal, Is Born](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/b/b4/Dolly_the_Sheep_with_her_First_Born_Bonnie.jpg)

## 1996-07-05

Dolly the sheep, the first mammal successfully cloned from an adult somatic cell, was born on July 5, 1996, at the Roslin Institute in Midlothian, Scotland. She was created by scientists Ian Wilmut and Keith Campbell through the groundbreaking process of somatic cell nuclear transfer. Her birth, initially kept secret, was publicly announced in February 1997 and immediately sparked widespread scientific and ethical debate about the implications of cloning technology. Dolly's existence provided definitive proof that specialized adult cells could be reprogrammed to create an entirely new, genetically identical organism. She lived for six years, producing several lambs, and profoundly advanced the field of genetic research and regenerative medicine.
