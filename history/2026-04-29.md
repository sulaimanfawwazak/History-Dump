# Los Angeles Riots Erupt After Rodney King Verdict

## 1992-04-29

On April 29, 1992, widespread riots erupted in Los Angeles, California, following the acquittal of four Los Angeles Police Department officers on charges of assault and excessive force in the beating of Rodney King. The verdict, delivered by a predominantly white jury, ignited simmering racial tensions and public outrage over perceived systemic injustice and police brutality. The unrest lasted for six days, resulting in over 60 deaths, thousands of injuries, and an estimated $1 billion in property damage. The riots highlighted profound societal divisions, sparking national discussions on race, poverty, and law enforcement practices. It remains one of the most significant civil disturbances in modern U.S. history.
