# Lindbergh Completes First Solo Transatlantic Flight

![Lindbergh Completes First Solo Transatlantic Flight](https://media.defense.gov/2020/Aug/20/2002481003/2000/2000/0/270920-F-9999X-1001.JPG)

## 1927-05-21

On May 21, 1927, American aviator Charles Lindbergh successfully completed the first solo non-stop transatlantic flight. Piloting his single-engine monoplane, the "Spirit of St. Louis," Lindbergh departed from Roosevelt Field in Long Island, New York, 33 and a half hours earlier. His historic journey concluded with a landing at Le Bourget Field near Paris, France, to the cheers of an enormous crowd of approximately 150,000 people. This monumental achievement instantly made Lindbergh a global hero and significantly boosted the public's interest and investment in aviation. The flight demonstrated the potential for long-distance air travel, forever changing the course of aviation history.
