## 1848-07-19

On July 19, 1848, the Seneca Falls Convention, the first women's rights convention in the United States, commenced in Seneca Falls, New York. Organized primarily by Elizabeth Cady Stanton and Lucretia Mott, the two-day event drew approximately 300 attendees, including some men like Frederick Douglass. The convention addressed social, civil, and religious conditions and rights of women. A central outcome was the drafting and adoption of the Declaration of Sentiments, which demanded equal social status and legal rights for women, including the right to vote. This landmark gathering is widely considered to have marked the beginning of the organized women's suffrage movement in America.
