## 1804-03-21

On March 21, 1804, the Napoleonic Code, officially known as the Code Civil des Français, was promulgated in France. This landmark civil code consolidated and codified French law, replacing the disparate and often confusing local laws that existed prior to the French Revolution. Championed by Napoleon Bonaparte, it established principles of equality before the law, protection of private property, and secularism, significantly influencing legal systems worldwide. The code's clear and systematically arranged articles provided a uniform legal framework, profoundly impacting civil law jurisdictions across Europe, the Americas, and beyond. Its legacy endures as one of Napoleon's most lasting and influential achievements.
