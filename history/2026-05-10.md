## 1994-05-10

On May 10, 1994, Nelson Mandela was officially inaugurated as the first black president of South Africa. The historic ceremony took place in Pretoria, marking the culmination of decades of struggle against apartheid and racial segregation. Mandela, who had spent 27 years imprisoned for his anti-apartheid activism, took the oath of office before a global audience and numerous world leaders. His presidency ushered in a new era of reconciliation and democratic governance for the nation, following its first multiracial elections. This momentous event celebrated the triumph of justice and equality, symbolizing hope for a unified South Africa.
