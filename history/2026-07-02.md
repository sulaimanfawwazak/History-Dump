# Civil Rights Act of 1964 Signed Into Law

![Civil Rights Act of 1964 Signed Into Law](https://cdn.britannica.com/88/136088-050-17171BF5/Pres-Lyndon-B-Johnson-others-Martin-Luther-July-2-1964.jpg)

## 1964-07-02

On July 2, 1964, President Lyndon B. Johnson signed the landmark Civil Rights Act into law during a ceremony at the White House. This monumental piece of legislation outlawed discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, or national origin. It specifically prohibited segregation in public accommodations, such as hotels and restaurants, and provided for the integration of schools and other public facilities. The act also made employment discrimination illegal and strengthened voting rights protections. Its passage marked a pivotal moment in the American Civil Rights Movement, fundamentally altering the social and legal landscape of the United States.
