# The Great San Francisco Earthquake Strikes

![The Great San Francisco Earthquake Strikes](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/0d/Post-and-Grant-Avenue-Look.jpg)

## 1906-04-18

On April 18, 1906, at approximately 5:12 AM Pacific Standard Time, a massive earthquake, estimated to be between 7.9 and 8.3 on the Richter scale, struck the coast of Northern California. The epicenter was near San Francisco, causing widespread devastation across the city and surrounding areas. While the initial tremor caused significant damage, the subsequent fires, fueled by ruptured gas lines and water main breaks, caused up to 90% of the total destruction. The disaster resulted in an estimated 3,000 deaths and left over 200,000 people homeless, making it one of the deadliest natural disasters in U.S. history.
