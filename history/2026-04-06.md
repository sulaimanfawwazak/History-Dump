## 1896-04-06

On April 6, 1896, the inaugural Olympic Games of the modern era commenced in Athens, Greece. This momentous event marked the revival of the ancient Olympic tradition, largely thanks to the efforts of French educator Pierre de Coubertin. Athletes from 14 nations competed in 43 events across 9 sports, including track and field, gymnastics, swimming, and wrestling. The opening ceremony took place at the Panathenaic Stadium, before a crowd of over 80,000 spectators, signaling a new chapter in international sports and cooperation.
