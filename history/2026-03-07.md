# Germany Reoccupies the Rhineland

![Germany Reoccupies the Rhineland](https://www.nationalarchives.gov.uk/wp-content/uploads/2021/11/large_000000.jpg)

## 1936-03-07

On March 7, 1936, German troops marched into the Rhineland, a demilitarized zone along Germany's western border, in a direct violation of the Treaty of Versailles and the Locarno Treaties. Adolf Hitler ordered the move, defying warnings from his generals about potential French or British military response. The reoccupation was met with little resistance from the Western powers, who pursued a policy of appeasement. This audacious act significantly boosted Hitler's popularity in Germany and marked a critical step in his aggressive foreign policy leading up to World War II. It exposed the weakness of the League of Nations and the reluctance of France and Britain to enforce the treaties.
