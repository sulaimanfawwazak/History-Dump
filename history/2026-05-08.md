## 1945-05-08

On May 8, 1945, the Allied nations celebrated Victory in Europe Day, marking the formal end of World War II in the European theater. The surrender of Nazi Germany to the Western Allies and the Soviet Union had been signed the previous day, coming into effect on this date. This monumental occasion was met with widespread celebrations across Europe and North America, signifying the defeat of the Third Reich and the culmination of years of devastating conflict. While the war continued in the Pacific, V-E Day brought immense relief and hope for peace to millions after nearly six years of global warfare.
