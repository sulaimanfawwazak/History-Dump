## 1940-07-10

The Battle of Britain, a pivotal aerial campaign of World War II, commenced on this day as the German Luftwaffe launched large-scale attacks against British airfields and radar installations. Adolf Hitler's objective was to gain air superiority over the Royal Air Force (RAF) as a prerequisite for a planned invasion of Britain, codenamed Operation Sea Lion. Despite being outnumbered, the RAF successfully defended the United Kingdom through tactical innovations and the bravery of its pilots. This prolonged aerial conflict marked the first major military campaign fought entirely by air forces and ultimately ended in a decisive British victory by October 1940. It significantly thwarted Germany's invasion plans and boosted Allied morale.
