## 1804-07-11

On July 11, 1804, former United States Secretary of the Treasury Alexander Hamilton engaged in a duel with his long-time political rival, Vice President Aaron Burr. The confrontation took place in Weehawken, New Jersey, across the Hudson River from New York City. The duel was the culmination of years of personal and political animosity between the two prominent American figures, fueled by political differences and perceived insults. Hamilton was shot in the abdomen and died the following day, marking a tragic end to the life of a key Founding Father. This event had significant political repercussions and left a lasting impact on early American history.
