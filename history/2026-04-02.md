## 1982-04-02

On April 2, 1982, Argentinian forces launched a surprise invasion of the Falkland Islands, a British overseas territory in the South Atlantic. This bold move by the military junta ruling Argentina was an attempt to assert long-standing claims of sovereignty over the islands, which Argentina refers to as the Islas Malvinas. The invasion quickly led to the occupation of the capital, Stanley, and ignited a brief but intense military conflict known as the Falklands War. The United Kingdom, under Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher, swiftly responded by dispatching a naval task force to reclaim the islands, leading to a 74-day war.
