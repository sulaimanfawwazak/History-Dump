## 1431-05-30

On May 30, 1431, Joan of Arc, the French peasant girl who led the French army to several important victories during the Hundred Years' War, was burned at the stake in Rouen, France. Accused of heresy and witchcraft by an English-dominated ecclesiastical court, she was condemned despite her unwavering claims of divine guidance. Her execution, at only 19 years old, made her a martyr for the French cause. Centuries later, she was canonized as a saint by the Roman Catholic Church and remains a revered national heroine of France.
