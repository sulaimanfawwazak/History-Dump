## 1933-03-23

On March 23, 1933, the German Reichstag (parliament) passed the "Law to Remedy the Distress of People and Reich," commonly known as the Enabling Act. This pivotal legislation effectively granted Adolf Hitler and his cabinet the power to enact laws without the involvement of the Reichstag or the President, even if those laws deviated from the constitution. Passed under immense pressure and intimidation, following the Reichstag fire and the arrest of communist deputies, the act marked the legal foundation for the Nazi dictatorship. It dismantled Germany's democratic institutions and concentrated all legislative power in Hitler's hands, paving the way for totalitarian rule and the suppression of all opposition.
