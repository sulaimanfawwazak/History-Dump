# President Johnson Signs Civil Rights Act of 1968

![President Johnson Signs Civil Rights Act of 1968](https://cdn.mississippitoday.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/06003019/April11-Civil-Rights-Act.jpg)

## 1968-04-11

On April 11, 1968, U.S. President Lyndon B. Johnson signed the Civil Rights Act of 1968, also known as the Fair Housing Act, into law. This landmark legislation outlawed discrimination in housing sales, rentals, and financing based on race, religion, national origin, and sex. Its passage came in the wake of the assassination of Dr. Martin Luther King Jr., just a week prior, adding urgency to its enactment. The act was a crucial follow-up to the Civil Rights Act of 1964 and the Voting Rights Act of 1965, completing the legislative trilogy aimed at ending racial discrimination in the United States. Its immediate impact was to provide legal recourse for victims of housing discrimination, although its full effects on desegregation continue to evolve.
