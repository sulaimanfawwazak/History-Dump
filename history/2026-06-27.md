## 1954-06-27

On June 27, 1954, the Obninsk Nuclear Power Plant, located in Obninsk, Kaluga Oblast, Soviet Union, became the world's first nuclear power plant to generate electricity for a power grid. Designed by the Soviet physicist Igor Kurchatov, the plant had a net electrical output of 5 MW. Its successful operation marked a significant milestone in the peaceful use of atomic energy, demonstrating the feasibility of nuclear fission for electricity generation. Obninsk operated for nearly 48 years, finally shutting down in April 2002.
