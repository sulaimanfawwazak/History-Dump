# First American Spacewalk Achieved by Edward H. White II

## 1965-06-03

On June 3, 1965, astronaut Edward H. White II made history by becoming the first American to perform a spacewalk, or Extravehicular Activity (EVA). During NASA's Gemini 4 mission, White spent 23 minutes outside the spacecraft, tethered by an umbilical line and maneuvering with a handheld propulsion gun. This groundbreaking achievement occurred over the Pacific Ocean, observed by his commander James McDivitt from inside the capsule. The spacewalk was a critical step for the United States in the Space Race, demonstrating the capability for astronauts to operate outside their vehicles in the vacuum of space. It paved the way for future complex space missions, including lunar landings.
