# Martin Luther King Jr. Assassinated In Memphis

## 1968-04-04

On April 4, 1968, civil rights leader Martin Luther King Jr. was assassinated at the Lorraine Motel in Memphis, Tennessee. He was standing on the motel's balcony when he was struck by a sniper's bullet. King had traveled to Memphis to support a sanitation workers' strike, advocating for better wages and working conditions. His death sparked riots and protests in cities across the United States, further highlighting the deep racial tensions and injustices of the era. The assassination remains a pivotal and tragic moment in American history, marking the end of an era of nonviolent protest led by King.
