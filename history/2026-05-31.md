# Johnstown Flood

![Johnstown Flood](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/71/Johnstown_flood_debris.jpg)

## 1889-05-31

On May 31, 1889, the city of Johnstown, Pennsylvania, was devastated by a catastrophic flood following the collapse of the South Fork Dam. Located 14 miles upstream on the Conemaugh River, the earthen dam failed after several days of unusually heavy rainfall, releasing an estimated 20 million tons of water. The dam, originally constructed for the Pennsylvania Main Line Canal, had been poorly maintained by its wealthy owners, the South Fork Fishing and Hunting Club. The ensuing deluge swept through the valley, annihilating towns in its path before hitting Johnstown with immense force, reaching speeds up to 40 miles per hour and heights of 60 feet in some areas. This disaster resulted in the deaths of over 2,200 people, including 99 entire families, making it one of the deadliest floods in United States history and prompting significant national relief efforts.
