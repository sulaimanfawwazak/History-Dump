## 1931-05-01

On May 1, 1931, the iconic Empire State Building officially opened its doors in New York City. U.S. President Herbert Hoover symbolically dedicated the skyscraper by pressing a button from Washington D.C., illuminating the building's lights for the first time. At 1,250 feet tall, it was the world's tallest building at the time, a title it held for nearly 40 years. Construction was completed in just over a year, ahead of schedule and under budget, during the Great Depression. The building quickly became a global symbol of American ingenuity and modern architectural achievement.
