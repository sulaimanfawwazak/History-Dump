# International Women's Day Protest Ignites February Revolution

![International Women's Day Protest Ignites February Revolution](https://lookaside.instagram.com/seo/google_widget/crawler/?media_id=3583231350121407024)

## 1917-03-08

On March 8, 1917 (February 23 Old Style), women textile workers in Petrograd, Russia, went on strike and demonstrated for 'Bread and Peace.' This protest, held on International Women's Day, quickly grew into a massive uprising as other workers and citizens joined the demonstrations. The escalating unrest and widespread calls for an end to the war and autocratic rule ultimately led to the abdication of Tsar Nicholas II just days later. This event is widely considered the beginning of the February Revolution, which overthrew the imperial government and paved the way for the Bolshevik Revolution later that year.
