# Tunguska Event Occurs in Siberia

## 1908-06-30

On June 30, 1908, a powerful explosion occurred near the Podkamennaya Tunguska River in what is now Krasnoyarsk Krai, Russia. This mysterious event, known as the Tunguska Event, flattened an estimated 80 million trees over an area of 2,150 square kilometers (830 sq mi) and registered on seismic stations across Eurasia. The blast's energy was equivalent to about 10–15 megatons of TNT. It is widely believed to have been caused by the air burst of a large meteoroid or comet fragment, rather than an impact with the ground, as no impact crater was ever found. Despite extensive scientific research, the exact nature of the celestial object remains a subject of ongoing study.
