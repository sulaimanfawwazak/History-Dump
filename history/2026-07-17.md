# Execution of Tsar Nicholas II and His Family

![Execution of Tsar Nicholas II and His Family](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/50/Ipatyev_house_basement.jpg)

## 1918-07-17

On July 17, 1918, Tsar Nicholas II, the last Emperor of Russia, along with his wife Empress Alexandra, their five children, and four loyal retainers, were brutally executed by Bolshevik revolutionaries. The killings occurred in the early hours in the basement of the Ipatiev House in Yekaterinburg, where the imperial family had been imprisoned. This act brought a definitive and violent end to the 300-year-old Romanov dynasty, which had ruled Russia since 1613. The execution was reportedly ordered by the Ural Regional Soviet to prevent the family's possible rescue by advancing White Army forces during the Russian Civil War. This tragic event solidified Bolshevik power and remains a dark and pivotal moment in Russian history.
