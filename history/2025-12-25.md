# Charlemagne Crowned Emperor of the Romans

![Charlemagne Crowned Emperor of the Romans](https://cdn.britannica.com/34/152534-050-F10EF781/Leo-III-emperor-Charlemagne-December-25.jpg)

## 0800-12-25

On Christmas Day in 800 AD, Charlemagne, King of the Franks and Lombards, was crowned "Emperor of the Romans" by Pope Leo III at Old St. Peter's Basilica in Rome. This momentous event marked the official revival of the Western Roman Empire and signified a profound shift in European political and religious power dynamics. Charlemagne's reign brought a period of cultural and intellectual revitalization known as the Carolingian Renaissance. The coronation established a precedent for the relationship between papacy and empire that would shape European history for centuries.
