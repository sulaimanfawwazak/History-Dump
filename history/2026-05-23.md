# Annulment of King Henry VIII's Marriage to Catherine of Aragon

![Annulment of King Henry VIII's Marriage to Catherine of Aragon](http://www.theanneboleynfiles.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Catherine-of-Aragon-and-Henry.jpg)

## 1533-05-23

On May 23, 1533, Archbishop Thomas Cranmer declared the marriage of King Henry VIII of England and Catherine of Aragon null and void. This significant ruling, delivered at a special court held at Dunstable Priory, effectively ended their 24-year union. The annulment was a pivotal moment, clearing the path for Henry VIII to marry Anne Boleyn in his pursuit of a male heir. This event was a major catalyst in the English Reformation, ultimately leading to England's break from papal authority and the establishment of the Church of England.
