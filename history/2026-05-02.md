## 1945-05-02

On May 2, 1945, the Battle of Berlin officially ended as German forces in the city surrendered to the Soviet Army. This pivotal engagement, which commenced on April 16, marked the culmination of the European theater of World War II. After weeks of brutal street fighting and significant casualties, the Red Army secured the German capital. The fall of Berlin was a profound strategic and psychological victory for the Allies, severely weakening the Nazi regime and directly leading to Germany's unconditional surrender a few days later.
