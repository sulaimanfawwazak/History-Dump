## 1914-06-28

On June 28, 1914, Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria, heir presumptive to the Austro-Hungarian throne, and his wife Sophie, Duchess of Hohenberg, were assassinated in Sarajevo, the capital of the Austro-Hungarian province of Bosnia and Herzegovina. The assassination was carried out by Gavrilo Princip, a Serbian nationalist and member of the Black Hand secret society. This act of political violence profoundly shocked Europe and served as the immediate catalyst for the outbreak of World War I. Austria-Hungary issued an ultimatum to Serbia, which ultimately escalated into a series of declarations of war among the European powers, igniting the global conflict that reshaped the 20th century.
