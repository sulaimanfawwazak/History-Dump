## 1954-05-17

On May 17, 1954, the U.S. Supreme Court delivered a unanimous 9-0 decision in the landmark case of Brown v. Board of Education of Topeka, Kansas. The Court ruled that racial segregation of children in public schools was unconstitutional, overturning the 'separate but equal' doctrine established by Plessy v. Ferguson in 1896. Chief Justice Earl Warren's opinion stated that 'separate educational facilities are inherently unequal,' directly challenging the legal basis for segregation in over 20 states. This pivotal ruling laid the legal groundwork for the desegregation of schools and was a major victory for the Civil Rights Movement, profoundly impacting American society and future civil rights legislation.
