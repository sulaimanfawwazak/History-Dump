# William Penn Receives Charter For Pennsylvania

![William Penn Receives Charter For Pennsylvania](https://www.phmc.state.pa.us/portal/communities/documents/images/charter1_1.jpg)

## 1681-03-04

On March 4, 1681, King Charles II of England granted William Penn, a prominent Quaker, a royal charter for a vast tract of land in North America. This grant was largely in settlement of a significant debt owed by the Crown to Penn's father, Admiral Sir William Penn. Penn envisioned this new territory as a "Holy Experiment," a haven for Quakers and other persecuted religious groups seeking freedom from religious oppression. The land was subsequently named Pennsylvania, meaning "Penn's Woods," in honor of his father. This foundational act led to the establishment of a colony unique for its principles of religious tolerance, democratic governance, and peaceful relations with indigenous peoples.
