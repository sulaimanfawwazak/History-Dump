# Marco Polo Bridge Incident Triggers Second Sino-Japanese War

![Marco Polo Bridge Incident Triggers Second Sino-Japanese War](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/46/Marco_Polo_Bridge_air_view.PNG)

## 1937-07-07

On July 7, 1937, the Marco Polo Bridge Incident occurred near Beijing, serving as the pretext for the Second Sino-Japanese War. Japanese troops conducting night maneuvers near the bridge exchanged fire with Chinese Nationalist forces following a Japanese soldier's temporary disappearance. Although the soldier soon returned, Japan used the incident to demand concessions and subsequently launched a full-scale invasion. This event marked the official beginning of a brutal and protracted conflict between China and Japan, which eventually merged into the broader scope of World War II. The incident significantly escalated tensions, leading to widespread devastation and immense human cost across East Asia for eight years.
