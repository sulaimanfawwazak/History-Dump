# Three Mile Island Nuclear Accident

![Three Mile Island Nuclear Accident](https://cdn.britannica.com/06/117706-050-F15B3FAC/view-Three-Mile-Island-Pennsylvania-Harrisburg.jpg)

## 1979-03-28

On March 28, 1979, the United States experienced its most serious commercial nuclear power plant incident at the Three Mile Island Generating Station near Harrisburg, Pennsylvania. A combination of equipment malfunctions, design flaws, and human error led to a partial meltdown of the reactor core in Unit 2. Although no immediate deaths or injuries were reported, and the release of radioactive materials was minimal, the event caused widespread panic and a mass evacuation of residents. This accident profoundly impacted public perception of nuclear energy, leading to significant changes in nuclear safety regulations and a long-term decline in new nuclear power plant construction in the U.S.
