# Egypt-Israel Peace Treaty Signed

## 1979-03-26

On March 26, 1979, the historic Egypt-Israel Peace Treaty was signed at the White House in Washington, D.C. This landmark agreement formally ended the state of war that had existed between the two nations since 1948. Egyptian President Anwar Sadat and Israeli Prime Minister Menachem Begin, having received the Nobel Peace Prize in 1978 for their efforts, signed the treaty with U.S. President Jimmy Carter as a witness. The treaty established full diplomatic and commercial relations, leading to Israel's withdrawal from the Sinai Peninsula. It marked a pivotal moment as the first peace treaty between Israel and an Arab nation, significantly altering the geopolitical landscape of the Middle East.
