# First Telegraphic Message Sent

![First Telegraphic Message Sent](https://www.loc.gov/exhibits/treasures/images/uc004059.jpg)

## 1844-05-24

On May 24, 1844, Samuel Morse sent the first official telegraph message from the U.S. Capitol Building in Washington D.C. to the B&O Railroad Depot in Baltimore, Maryland. Using his innovative Morse code system, the historic phrase "What hath God wrought?" was transmitted, signifying a groundbreaking achievement in communication. This successful demonstration, funded by a U.S. Congressional appropriation, validated the practical application of electrical telegraphy. It marked the dawn of rapid long-distance communication, laying the essential foundation for global information exchange and revolutionizing commerce, journalism, and personal correspondence.
