# Fall of Constantinople

## 1453-05-29

On May 29, 1453, the Ottoman Empire, under the leadership of Sultan Mehmed II, successfully breached the formidable defenses of Constantinople, bringing an end to the Byzantine Empire. The siege, which lasted for 53 days, culminated in a final assault where the Ottoman forces overwhelmed the vastly outnumbered Byzantine defenders. Emperor Constantine XI Palaiologos died fighting in the city's defense, marking the definitive end of the Roman Empire's eastern continuation. This pivotal event profoundly altered the course of European and Middle Eastern history, establishing the city as the new capital of the Ottoman Empire, renamed Istanbul. It is widely considered by historians to mark the end of the Middle Ages and the beginning of the early modern period.
